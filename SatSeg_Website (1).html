Overview

What This Research Does

An end-to-end deep learning pipeline that segments building footprints from satellite imagery with state-of-the-art accuracy across geographically diverse cities.

Accurate building detection from satellite imagery underpins urban planning, disaster response, population estimation, and geospatial intelligence at scale.

Traditional U-Net architectures over-segment non-building regions (roads, parking lots) with roof-like textures. This research introduces two targeted improvements:

① Soft Attention Gates at every decoder skip connection — the model learns which spatial locations matter, suppressing irrelevant activations.

② Spatial Dropout (p=0.3) applied after the final decoder block — stochastically zeroes entire feature maps, preventing co-adaptation and improving generalization across unseen cities.

Trained on the Inria Aerial Image Labeling Dataset — 360 high-resolution tiles spanning 5 cities — and benchmarked against FCN-8s, SegNet, DeepLab v3+, and vanilla U-Net.

🛰️ Multi-city generalization

Trained on Austin, Chicago, Kitsap, Western Tyrol, Vienna. Evaluated on held-out tiles to test geographic generalization.

🎯 State-of-the-art on Inria

Outperforms all four baselines across pixel accuracy, IoU, F1, Dice, and AUC — with +6.1% IoU improvement over vanilla U-Net.

⚡ Efficient inference

7.4M parameters. 18ms per 256×256 patch on NVIDIA T4. Full 5000×5000 satellite tile in ~30 seconds.
